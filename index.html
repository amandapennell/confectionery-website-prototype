· Our Story ·

Our story starts in Southhampton, England in 1812. Charlotte McCool was a young woman tired of not having her dream of owning a chocolate shop taken seriously. She had just been rejected by yet another financier and was dejectedly roaming the city. A piece of paper blew past Charlotte, which she discovered was a ticket for a ship headed to New York City. Noticing that the ship was minutes away from departure and unlikely to find its owner, Charlotte ran to the docks to start a new life.

This ship, was of course the RMS Titanic, which would have its illustrious voyage cut short when it collided with an iceberg on its way across the Atlantic Ocean. Charlotte was extremely fortunate to have survived the sinking, but her treasured chocolate recipes had been lost with the ship - or so she thought.

A few days later, Charlotte received a telegram that her recipe book had been found on one of the rescue ships. Charlotte unexpectedly fell in love with the man who found her book, William "Beau" Longstreth, and they were married. Beau and Charlotte moved to Ottawa, Canada where in 1914, Charlotte's dream of owning a chocolate store finally came to life.

To this day, Beau's Bonbons remains one of the Byward Market's most beloved stores. Our chocolates are always made in-house using Charlotte's original recipes and we are honoured to keep Charlotte's dream alive.
